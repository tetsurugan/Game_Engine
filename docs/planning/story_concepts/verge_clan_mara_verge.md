## Core premise

**Mara Verge** is the **irreplaceable living interpreter** of the **Verge clan**—not merely powerful in the usual sense, but the **embodied timing, memory, and instinctive rulebook** of a group that never fully externalized how it survives.

- The clan’s “law” is not a document. It is **when to speak**, **when to vanish**, **who owes silence**, **how to read a room that is full of enemies pretending to be creditors**. That knowledge lives in **Mara’s nervous system**—accumulated through years of **correct guesses** and **survived mistakes**.
- **If she dies unchanged** (knowledge never passed, never translated into teachable pattern): the clan **imitates** her past decisions without the feel. It becomes **costume**—ritual without judgment. **Slow collapse into parody**, then prey.
- **If she survives but does not evolve or teach**: the clan **depends** on her presence forever. When she weakens or withdraws, **nothing holds**. **Late collapse**—often uglier than sudden death, because dependency masked the hollow center.
- **If she evolves and deliberately passes the fire** (teaches, mutates the code, accepts impurity in the successor): the clan **survives another generation in an altered form**—not the same Verge, but a **living** Verge.
- **If she partially transfers** (half-teaching, chosen heir rejects part of the fire, rival interpretations): the clan **splits**—**unstable**, **factional**, sometimes **stronger in pieces**, often **destroyed by internal contradiction**.

---

## Why she matters

- **Writing fails:** Anything put on paper or in a ledger becomes **evidence**, **leverage**, or **misread copy**. The Verge survive by **context** that does not survive transcription—**who was in the room**, **what was not said**, **which silence meant yes**.
- **Hierarchy fails:** A council or CEO can **execute**, not **interpret**. The clan’s edge is **judgment under ambiguity**. Replacing Mara with **title** replaces **timing** with **procedure**—and rivals learn procedures.
- **She is the checksum:** Outsiders see “family business” or “old network.” Insiders know Mara is the **last node** that still **matches** the living graph. Remove her without **transfer**, and the graph **corrupts**—slowly or at once.

## Branch outcomes

Four **major** outcome families (not necessarily one ending each—engine may map several ending ids onto these **consequence profiles**).

### 1. Untranslated death

| | |
|--|--|
| **Immediate** | Mara dies (violence, betrayal, accident, or sacrifice) with **no** successor who holds the **full interpretive load**—or the successor is **proclaimed** but **not forged**. |
| **Long-term clan** | **Imitation phase** → **fragmentation** → absorption or war. False heirs, **cargo-cult** ritual, **predation** by rivals who studied the gap. |
| **Unlocks** | Aftershocks: **false heir**, **rival observers of decline**, **descendant reconstruction**, **anti-clan offshoot** claiming “pure Verge.” **Witness / documentary** modules on **what was lost**. |
| **Blocks** | Clean **continuation** of “Mara’s Verge” as same entity; **dependency-collapse** path (she never got to fail by living too long); some **passing-of-fire** sequels unless a **retroactive** teaching beat is written as **prequel** flashback module. |

### 2. Survival without evolution

| | |
|--|--|
| **Immediate** | Mara lives; **wins** or **endures**; **does not** teach, delegate interpretive depth, or accept a changed clan shape. **Centralization** on her. |
| **Long-term clan** | **Dependency**. Younger Verge **execute** well but **cannot read** the whole board. When Mara fades, **sudden** or **creeping** collapse—**worse** than death in some timelines because **hope** lasted longer. |
| **Unlocks** | **Dependency collapse** module; **succession crisis** timed to her illness or disappearance; **observer** modules (“we knew it would hollow”). |
| **Blocks** | **Clean fire-passed** endings without retcon; **stable split-lineage** play unless written as **late fracture**. |

### 3. Passing of fire

| | |
|--|--|
| **Immediate** | Mara **chooses** and **trains** a successor (or **small circle**) and **accepts** mutation—new edges, new compromises. **Ritual or non-ritual** transfer completes enough to **checksum** the clan. |
| **Long-term clan** | **Altered but living** Verge. New interpreter(s); **different** mistakes; **continuity** of **judgment culture**, not of **Mara’s person**. |
| **Unlocks** | **Direct continuation** candidate: **heir’s first crisis**; **variant** swaps in political / duel / social modules (“Verge still here, different face”). **Soft** mythic echoes if the transfer becomes **legend**. |
| **Blocks** | **Untranslated-death** mutex echoes on same profile (unless multiverse / alt profile—out of scope unless designed). **Fake heir rises** as **primary** threat is **reduced** if transfer was **sound**—may downgrade to **personal** rumor. |

### 4. Incomplete transfer

| | |
|--|--|
| **Immediate** | Teaching **started**; **stopped**; **two** (or more) **competing** interpretations; or heir **accepts power** but **rejects** part of the fire. |
| **Long-term clan** | **Split lineage**—parallel Verge claims, **civil** conflict, **external** exploitation of the seam. |
| **Unlocks** | **Split lineage war** stories; **negotiation / witness** modules; **fake successor who unexpectedly works** (illegitimate line **stabilizes**); **rival group** studying **Verge schism**. |
| **Blocks** | **Monolithic** “the Verge” as a single gate for some modules—design should use **`verge_split_lineage`** or faction flags to **branch** variants. |

## Aftershock / follow-up modules

Ideas mapped to **branch families** (per [`branch_scope_doctrine.md`](../branch_scope_doctrine.md), most are **aftershock** or **witness**, not all **direct continuations**).

### After untranslated death

| Module idea | Hook |
|-------------|------|
| **False heirs** | Competing claimants mimic Mara; **cargo-cult** violence. |
| **Descendant reconstruction** | Grandchild or outsider **rebuilds** from fragments—**witness** + **investigation** template. |
| **Rival observers** | Corp, state, or enemy clan **studies** the Verge **vacuum**—**observer_documentary** or **court** frame. |
| **Anti-clan offshoot** | “We are the **real** memory” — **mutually_exclusive** with imitation Verge. |

### After survival without evolution

| Module idea | Hook |
|-------------|------|
| **Dependency collapse** | Mara absent or diminished; **institutional** panic; **pressured** or **possessed**-leaning panic POVs. |
| **False heir under pressure** | Someone **must** play Mara; **distorted** perception of their own competence. |

### After passing of fire

| Module idea | Hook |
|-------------|------|
| **Heir’s crucible** | First **big** judgment call without Mara—**narrative** or **witness** trial. |
| **Fake successor who works** | Illegitimate or **rejected** line **stabilizes** clan—**irony** sequel; **variant** intro. |

### After incomplete transfer

| Module idea | Hook |
|-------------|------|
| **Split lineage conflict** | Two Verge **courts**, two **silences**—**duel_tournament** or **political** template. |
| **Transformed anti-clan** | Faction **rejects** Mara’s compromise—**mythic-leaning** echo possible. |

3. **Phase C:** **`startable`** — full intro; **`continuationOf`** may point to `verge_clan_mara` for heir modules.

## Themes

- **Legacy** — what survives a person vs what survives a **name**.
- **Irreplaceability** — the **cost** of centrality.
- **Knowledge dying with a person** — **untranslated** loss.
- **Dependency** — love and **structural** need tangled.
- **Transmission vs purity** — teaching **dilutes** or **saves**; purity **kills** or **freezes**.
- **Clan survival through mutation** — the Verge **must** change to live.
- **The last true interpreter** — Mara as **checksum**, not **saint**.
